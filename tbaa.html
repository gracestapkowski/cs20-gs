ACTIVITIES/ATTRACTIONS

The Freedom Trail

Description

The Freedom Trail is currently Boston's most popular tourist attraction. It is a 2.5 mile trail through the streets of Boston passing through 16 nationally significant historical sites! On the trail, you can experience more than 250 years of history that has been preserved since 1951. Some of the stops on the trail include the Boston Common, the Massachussetts State House, the Park Street Church, and the Old State House. You can also visit the very spot that the Boston Massacre happened, as well as the midnight ride of Paul Revere!

The Museum of Fine Arts

Description

The Museum of Fine Arts is the 20th-largest museum in the world, containing 8,161 paintings and more than 450,000 works of art. In fact, it will likely take you at least two days to experience everything that this museum has to offer. Some current exhibitions include "Hear Me Now: The Black Potters of Old Edgefield, South Carolina" and "Making Past Present: Cy Twombly". Become one of the 1.2 million visitors that the museum has every year, today!

Students at Tufts University and their School of the Museum of Fine Arts gain free admittence to the museum, as well as people with an existing membership. There are also many travelling exhibits that come to the museum, which may require an additional purchase.

Boston Public Garden

Description

Established in 1837, the Public Garden was the first botanical garden in America. The garden has over 80 species of plants, and during the warmer months, you can see many ducks living in the pond. There are also several statues around the garden, such as an equestrian statue of George Washington, the "Small Child Fountain" and "The Ether Monument".

Also at the public garden, there are available Swan Boat Tours which are $4.50 for adults, $3.00 for children ages 2-15 (under 2 ride free), and $4.00 for seniors (65+)

Boston Tea Party Ships and Museum

Description

Voted #1 "Best Patriotic Attraction", at this museum you can be a part of the event that forever changed the course of American history! You can re-enact the event through historical interpreters, interactive exhibits, and full-scale replica 18th-century sailing vessels. There are five main sections to the museum: the meeting house, tea party ships, griffin's wharf, reenactment in 3D, the robinson tea chest, and the minuteman theater, all of which will give you unique insight into the iconic piece of American history.

Quincy Market

Description

There are hundreds of shops both inside and outside the Quincy Market for all of your shopping needs. Open since 1826, its role has changed from fresh produce retail to a festival marketplace. It features food from all around the globe and lots of shops selling homemade jewelry to giant squishmallows.

New England Aquarium

Description

The New England Aquarium is home to thousands of aquatic animals with a large range of exhibits. They have the largest shark and ray touch tank on the East Coast and a four-stoey coral reef home to a green sea turtle named "Myrtle" and more carribean animals.
